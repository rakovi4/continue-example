Four things the run settles

The spine is not where the time is

17.4% of agent work, and green-agent is 2.2%. Even a perfect spine — zero seconds — leaves 82.6% of the run standing.

The passes cost only what they fail to overlap

Settled at n=6. Behind a working /refactor: 0–103s. With nothing to hide behind: ~330s. Only design and green-acceptance pay full price.

T3 is aimed at the wrong variable

WU11’s compaction was an accidental controlled experiment: dispatch stagger tracks the caller’s context size, not prompt length. T3 proposes a prompt-length fix.

A gate restructured half the run for 85 seconds

adapters-discovery dispatched zero agents, cost 1.4 minutes, and scheduled 48% of the remaining scenario. Gates aren’t expensive — fleets are.

The overlap rule — review passes’ marginal cost, n=6

The passes are not expensive. They are expensive when nothing overlaps them. This is why T6’s lever is the batch, not the passes.

Task 10 is 81% of the win

4131s of the 5099s. Everything else on that table is rounding — and Task 10 is the one thing the two blocked ADR decisions in Task 9 sit in front of.

D9’s projection is optimistic on both ends

It projected old 97m / new 36m = 2.7×. Measured old is 166m — 1.7× worse than projected. Modelled new is 81m. The real ratio is nearer 2.0×.

The floor nobody is attacking

The new path is still 7 units deep at ~900s each. No task in T1–T9 makes a single unit cheaper — they only take units off the path. Past ~2× that floor is the whole game.

Half the initiative is unmeasured

This covers backend scenarios only. T4 (Task 11, frontend chains) has no baseline — the frontend ceiling run is still open. Its impact is a guess.
